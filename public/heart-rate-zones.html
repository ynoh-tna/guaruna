Understanding the 5 heart-rate zones

  • Zone 1 — Recovery (50–60%): very easy, for warm-ups and active recovery.
  • Zone 2 — Endurance (60–70%): conversational pace; builds your aerobic base.
  • Zone 3 — Tempo (70–80%): comfortably hard, "marathon effort".
  • Zone 4 — Threshold (80–90%): hard, sustainable for ~1 hour.
  • Zone 5 — VO2 max (90–100%): very hard intervals.

%HRmax vs. Karvonen

The %HRmax method takes a percentage of your maximum heart rate. The Karvonen method uses your heart-rate reserve (max − resting), which accounts for fitness and usually gives slightly higher, more individual zone boundaries. If you know your resting HR, Karvonen is the better choice.

Note: 220 − age is a rough estimate. For real precision, use a max HR from a recent hard effort.

Frequently asked questions

How are the heart-rate zones calculated?

As a percentage of your maximum heart rate, or with the Karvonen method using your heart-rate reserve when you add a resting heart rate.

What if I do not know my max heart rate?

Leave it blank and it is estimated from your age as 220 minus your age.

What are the five zones?

Recovery, endurance, tempo, threshold and VO2 max, each shown as a heart-rate range in beats per minute.
