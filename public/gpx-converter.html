Which format do you need?

GeoJSON is the standard for web maps and GIS tools like QGIS, Mapbox and Leaflet. CSV drops every point into a spreadsheet (lat, lon, elevation, time) for analysis. KML opens your route in Google Earth and Google My Maps. TCX is Garmin's training format, handy for importing a route as an activity. If a timestamp is missing, the TCX export fills in evenly spaced times so the file stays valid.

Private by design

The conversion runs entirely in your browser with JavaScript — your GPX is never sent to a server. The map tiles come from OpenStreetMap; only the view you see is requested.

Need to trim or split first? Use the GPX editor. Want to shrink a heavy file? Try the GPX cleaner.

Frequently asked questions

Which formats can I convert a GPX to?

GeoJSON, CSV, KML and TCX, each with a single click.

How do I convert a GPX to KML for Google Earth?

Drop your GPX file and click the KML button. The download opens directly in Google Earth or Google My Maps.

Is it free and private?

Yes. The conversion runs entirely in your browser and your file is never uploaded.

My GPX has no timestamps, will the TCX still work?

Yes. The TCX export fills in evenly spaced times so the file stays valid for Garmin and other apps.
