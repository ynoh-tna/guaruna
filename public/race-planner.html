How to plan your race pace

Your average pace is simply your target finish time divided by the distance. For a sub-2-hour half marathon (21.1 km in 2:00:00), that's about 5:41 /km. The split table shows the cumulative time you should hit each kilometre at if you run an even pace — the most reliable way to race well.

Even vs. negative splits

Holding the same pace throughout is an even split. Many runners aim for a slight negative split (second half a touch faster), so treat the table as your ceiling for the first half.

Want to check a recorded run instead? Use the GPX viewer, or set your effort with heart-rate zones.

Frequently asked questions

How is my goal pace calculated?

It is your target finish time divided by the distance, shown as an even-split pace per kilometre.

Can I use any distance?

Yes. Pick a preset (5K, 10K, half, marathon) or enter any custom distance in kilometres or miles.

Is the race planner free?

Yes, free, no account, and it runs entirely in your browser.
